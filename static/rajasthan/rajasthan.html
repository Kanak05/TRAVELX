About Rajasthan


Rajasthan, Lands of Kings


Rajasthan is the largest state of the Republic of India in terms of area . For the first time in 1800 AD, George Thomas named this province Rajputana. Famous historian James Tad named this state as Raithan or Rajasthan in "Annals and Antiquities of Rajasthan"


The capital of the state is Jaipur. Geographical features include the Thar Desert in the west and the end of the Ghaggar River. The Aravalli range, which is prominent among the ancient ranges of the world, is the only mountain range of Rajasthan, which is a center of tourism, and includes Mount Abu and the world famous Delwara Temple . Rajasthan has three (four after the addition of Ramgarh Vishdhari) tiger reserves, Mukandra Hills , Ranthambore and Sariska and Keoladeo National Park near Bharatpur .


Jaipur


Jaipur [14] is famous for its grand forts, palaces and beautiful lakes, which attract tourists from all over the world. Chandramahal (City Palace) was built by Maharaja Jai ​​Singh (II) and is a combination of Mughal and Rajasthani architecture. Maharaja Sawai Pratap Singh built Hawa Mahal in 1799 AD, whose architect was Lalchand Usta. Amer Fort has many building complexes including palaces, huge chambers, pillared galleries, gardens and temples. Amer Palace is an excellent example of the blend of Mughal and Hindu architectural styles. The museum, named Albert Hall, was built by Sawai Ram Singh in 1876, on the arrival of the Prince of Wales to Jaipur, and opened to the public in 1886.
