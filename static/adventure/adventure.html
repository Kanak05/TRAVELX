ADVENTURE

PACKAGES

  • Hill Station In North India

    The hill stations of North India offer pleasant respite from scorching heat during summers and in winters, they become a place to enjoy adventure sports.

    3 Days

  • Gulmarg, Jammu & Kashmir

    This “Heaven on Earth” in Kashmir is globally acclaimed for its magical charm and is equally known for hosting some enthralling adventure sports in India. Gulmarg is one of the best adventure destinations to designate its name under adventure tourism in India.

    2-3 Days

  • Auli, Uttarakhand

    Once you land in Auli, you would feel like you’ve landed in a magical town straight out of a fairy tale! Embellished with dense coats of snow and thick coniferous jungles, this adventure destination owns a reputable image amongst the names in adventure tourism in India.

    2 Days

  • Andaman & Nicobar Islands

    Banana Boat Rides, Jet Skiing, Undersea Walking, Scuba Diving, Snorkelling, Parasailing, Kayaking, Seakart Adventure

    3 Days

  • Ladakh

    The Chadar Trek or The Frozen River Expedition is one of the most unique and challenging treks in India. The 6-day chadar trek across the frozen Zanskar River in Ladakh takes place during the latter part of January till the end of February or sometimes even in the first week of March, depending on the weather.

    2 Days

  • Aamby Valley, Maharastra

    Situated in Maharasthra, Aamby Valley is this exotic palace with a remarkable collection of experiences and attractions. Whether you travel to this adventure destination for a golf match or a wild safari, you’ll promisingly have it all there.

    3 Days

BIKING

  • Ladakh, Jammu and Kashmir

    Ladakh is the land of beautiful lakes, high mountain passes, Buddhist monasteries and meandering roads that are mostly unpaved. Therefore while planning a ride here, one is spoilt for choices but what you must know that you can never make a bad choice as each trail has its perks and thrills.

    Total Riding Distance: 1500 km approximately

    Best Riding Season: Mid June to Mid October

  • Kolli Hills, Tamil Nadu

    The hills are famed for its 70 continuous hairpin bends, which by the way are more than enough to get you all excited. This tropical hill is also famous for its Agaya Gangai waterfall, ancient Shiva temple and Pineapple farms. A weekend bike ride from Bangalore to Kolli Hills seems an ideal plan.

    Total Riding Distance: 1023 km approximately

    Best Riding Season: All year around

  • Kerala

    A motorbike holiday in Kerala is an unforgettable experience. Kerala is the land of pristine beaches, beautiful lighthouses and mesmerizing greenery. In the state, one is spoilt for choices when it comes to decide the bike route. Ideal trip in Kerala would include a bike ride from Cochin towards the Southern Ghats where a wide variety of wildlife can be seen.

    Total Riding Distance: 1499 km approximately

    Best Riding Season: All year around

  • Mumbai – Goa

    It is one of the most famous road trip routes in India and it deserves all the fame that it gets. Popularly called NH17 (National Highway 17), this route offers a trip along the western coastline of India. NH 17 connects Mumbai to Kerala, so in case your adventure instinct doesn’t get satiated with a ride between Mumbai and Goa, you can simply get some more petrol in your bike and vroom off to the state of Kerala.

    Total Riding Distance: 1600 km approximately

    Best Riding Season: October to February

  • Jaipur-Jodhpur-Jaisalmer Motorcycle Expedition

    By taking a bike tour, you will be able to explore the tiny nooks and corners of the city and learn about its’ history and heritage. For those interested in exploring beyond just nature, this is an apt bike tour in India to take.

    Total Riding Distance: 1723 km approximately

    Best Riding Season:November to February

  • Srinagar - Leh - Manali Motorcycle Expedition

    The highway road from Srinagar to Leh and Manali has its own sets of challenges. However, upon careful planning of the trip, you can ensure a stress-free and comfortable tour. Along the route, travellers will be passing through The Dal Lake, lush meadows of Kashmir and the Zojila Pass.

    Total Riding Distance: 1350 km approximately

    Best Riding Season:September and the start of October

PARA GLIDING

  • Bir-Billing: Best Paragliding Site in Asia

    Bir, nestled in Himachal Pradesh, is renowned as the Paragliding Capital of India, boasting a picturesque setting with stunning mountains and greenery. It offers a blend of serenity and adventure, attracting both nature enthusiasts and thrill-seekers. Bir serves as the take-off point while Billing, 14 km away, is the landing site.

    Best Time to Visit : October and mid-June

  • Panchgani, Maharashtra

    A famous hill station located in the Satara district of Maharashtra, Panchgani is one of the best weekend getaways one could probably ever think of. With an altitude of about 1200 m above the sea level, the fantastic hill station opens doors for some adventure sports, paragliding being one of the favourites of the tourists who frequent the area.

    Best Time to Visit : November to February

  • Kamshet, Maharashtra

    The place is well suited for paragliding again due to the favourable weather conditions, amazing view and perfect wind conditions. It is well suited for beginners, in addition to being connected to the Pune- Mumbai expressway; this place is great for a weekend getaway.

    Best Time to Visit : October to June

  • Vagamon, Kerala

    Located in the Idukki district of Kerala, the beautiful hill station is a dream for adventure enthusiasts. Temperatures ranging from 10- 23 degree Celsius on summer midday, this place is ideal for paragliding.

    Best Time to Visit : October to March

  • Pavagadh Hill in Gujarat

    Pavagadh Hill is the best spot to indulge in paragliding in Gujarat. It is located inside a plain in Panchmahal region of Gujarat and the slope is a southern Aravalli Range hill.

    Best Time to Visit : october to November

  • Sanasar in Jammu

    Sanasar is a beautiful tourist destination in Jammu for the nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts. It is around 19 kilometers away from Patnitop and is one of the best places in Jammu to experience paragliding.

    Best Time to Visit : June to July

SURFING

  • Kovalam Beach, Kerala

    Kovalam Beach in the South Indian state of Kerala is quite a famous tourist destination. Many beach bums find themselves on this beach on a regular basis to indulge in an array of activities. And guess what, among the top activities to do on this beach is surfing.

    Best Time to Visit : May and continues till October

  • Mahabalipuram, Tamil Nadu

    This temple town of Tamil Nadu needs no introduction, we know, as it is already under the spotlight for a large number of temples and religious spots it houses. But, allow us to give an introduction to Mahabalipuram’s surfing scenes! This is among the top places for sea surfing in India.

    Best Time to Visit : June or July

  • Pondicherry, Tamil Nadu

    This French Capital of India is dotted with mustard-yellow colonial buildings and vibrant bougainvillaeas. As much as we suggest a long sightseeing tour, let’s get to today’s point, surfing! For that, head to Serenity Beach.

    Best Time to Visit : June to January

  • Varkala, Kerala

    A Varkala surfing experience is a must for any ardent surfer! Don’t worry, even beginners can have a fun time here. Other than a famous beach break, many other surf breaks are there in close vicinity. Now if you are experienced, you are good to go. If not, you will need some lessons. This is where the name of Soul and Surf comes up.

    Best Time to Visit : early October till mid-May

  • Gokarna, Karnataka

    Pilgrims, backpackers, hippies, and wandering surfers all converge on the picturesque temple town of Gokarna in Karnataka. Its placid beach breaks make it a good place for beginners to learn to surf.

    Best Time to Visit : October to May

  • Goa

    Goa is known for its parties more than its surf. However, the coastline of this small state does have a few spots that serve up ideal waves for beginners. The Ashwem to Arambol stretch, in the far north, is your best bet.

    Best Time to Visit : October until April
